FLAVORFUL JOLLOF RICE

Description

Classic Nigerian jollof rice is a vibrant, one-pot dish bursting with color, aroma, and rich, smoky flavor. Long-grain parboiled rice is simmered in a luscious blend of ripe tomatoes, red bell peppers, onions, and fiery scotch bonnet, all slowly cooked down into a deeply seasoned sauce. Infused with garlic, ginger, and warming spices, the grains absorb every drop of the savory tomato base until they’re tender yet separate, with a hint of crispness at the bottom known as “party rice.” Served alongside fried plantains, grilled chicken, or fish, this beloved West African staple is not just a meal—it’s a celebration on a plate.

Servings and Time

Serves; 4 - 6 people | Prep; 15 minutes | Cook Time; 40 - 50 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice (e.g., Golden Sella or any non-sticky variety)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil (or any neutral cooking oil)
  • 1 large onion (half sliced, half blended)
  • 4 large tomatoes (blended) or 400g canned chopped tomatoes
  • 2 red bell peppers (blended)
  • 2–3 scotch bonnet peppers (blended; adjust to spice tolerance)
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 3–4 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1–2 tsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 2–3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp paprika (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Stock cubes or bouillon (optional, for extra flavor)

Procedure

  1. Prepare the pepper mix

    2. Blend tomatoes, red bell peppers, scotch bonnet, and half the onion into a smooth puree

  2. Cook down the sauce

    3. Heat oil in a large pot. Add sliced onions and sauté until soft. Stir in tomato paste and fry for 2–3 minutes.Add blended pepper mix. Cook on medium heat until it reduces and the oil starts separating (about 15–20 mins).

  3. Season the base

    4. Stir in curry powder, thyme, paprika, bay leaves, salt, and stock cubes. Pour in chicken/veg stock and bring to a boil.

  4. Add the rice

    5. Rinse rice to remove excess starch, then add it to the pot. Stir gently so it’s evenly coated in the sauce.

  5. Steam to perfection

    6. Reduce heat to low. Cover with foil or baking paper under the lid (to trap steam). Let it cook for 25–30 mins, stirring once halfway. Add a splash of stock or water if it’s too dry.

  6. Final touch

    7. Once rice is cooked and fluffy, stir gently to mix. Adjust seasoning if needed.

Tips & Notes

Use long-grain parboiled rice to avoid mushiness. Cook the pepper base until the oil separates for maximum flavor. Adjust scotch bonnet to control heat. If using fresh tomatoes, drain excess water after blending to reduce cooking time.

Serving Suggestions

Serve hot with fried plantains, grilled chicken, beef, or fish. You can also pair it with coleslaw or a simple green salad.

CLASSIC UGANDAN ROLEX

Description

A popular East African street food, the Ugandan Rolex is a tasty and portable wrap made with soft chapati, spiced eggs, and fresh vegetables. Simple to prepare and full of flavor, it’s perfect for breakfast, lunch, or a quick snack.

Servings and Time

Serves 2–3 people | Prep: 10 mins | Cook: 15 mins

Ingredients

  • 2–3 chapatis (store-bought or homemade)
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 medium tomato, finely chopped
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 green bell pepper, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 small carrot, grated (optional)
  • 1–2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • Black pepper or pilipili (chili), to taste

Procedure

  1. Crack the eggs into a bowl, add chopped tomato, onion, bell pepper, and carrot. Season with salt and pepper. Beat well.
  2. Heat a tablespoon of oil in a frying pan over medium heat.
  3. Pour in a portion of the egg mixture (enough to make a thin omelette)
  4. Place a chapati directly on top of the egg while it’s still cooking, gently pressing so they stick together.
  5. Flip carefully so the chapati side touches the pan, cooking for another 30–60 seconds.
  6. Remove from the pan and roll the chapati and egg together tightly.
  7. Repeat with the remaining egg mixture and chapatis.

Tips & Notes

For extra flavor, add chopped fresh coriander or a pinch of curry powder to the eggs. If you prefer, cook the omelette separately and roll it with the chapati afterwards.

Serving Suggestions

Serve hot as a quick breakfast, a roadside snack, or with a side of tea or fresh juice.
